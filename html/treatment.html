Water Treatment

PFAS can be removed from drinking water with two types of filters: activated carbon and reverse osmosis.

  • Activated carbon includes solid carbon block filters (typically a filter under a sink) and granular activated carbon (such as a pitcher filter).
  • Reverse osmosis systems are more effective at removing PFAS. However, reverse osmosis systems can be more expensive and create a lot of wastewater.

    • Look for a filter that is certified by the National Science Foundation (NSF) to remove PFAS. Follow the manufacturer's instructions and replace the filters as recommended. Visit the Massachusetts DEP website for more information about water treatment and the safety of bottled water.
